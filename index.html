Skills
My Technical Skills

  • Estimating & Costing

    This skill involves calculating project costs, including materials, labor, equipment, and overhead. Accurate cost estimation helps in budgeting, bidding, and financial planning. Engineers balance quality and cost-effectiveness while managing financial risks throughout the project.

  • Surveying & Mapping

    Surveying involves measuring land and mapping topography to guide construction planning. Engineers use modern tools like total stations, GPS, and drones to create accurate site maps, which help in the design and layout of roads, buildings, and infrastructure.

  • Construction Management

    Construction management focuses on planning, coordinating, and overseeing construction projects. Civil engineers manage resources, schedules, and budgets to ensure timely project completion. They also supervise contractors and ensure quality control and adherence to safety regulations on site.

  • Building codes & Standards

    Civil engineers must understand and apply local and international building codes and standards. These regulations ensure that structures meet safety, health, and performance requirements. Engineers design and review projects to comply with all relevant legal requirements.

  • Geo-Technical Engineering

    Geotechnical engineering deals with understanding soil and rock behavior to design stable foundations. Engineers conduct site investigations, analyze soil samples, and recommend suitable foundation types to prevent issues like settling, landslides, or structural failures

  • AutoCAD

    I use AutoCAD software to create precise 2D drawings and 3D models of engineering designs. These drawings include plans, elevations, sections, and details for structures, roads, drainage systems, and utilities. AutoCAD allows me to produce clear, accurate, and professional drawings that are essential for construction, permits, and client presentations.
